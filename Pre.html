Helminth Infection and Immune Response

Type 2 Immune Response

  • TH2 cell
  • Massive stimulation of antibody production
  • IL-4, IL-13, IL-5, IL-9
  • B-cell class-switching from IgG to IgE
  • Eosinophilic and basophilic tissue infiltration
  • Extensive mast cell degranulation (IL-9)

Strongyloides ratti

  • Rodent-specific parasitic nematode.
  • Laboratory analogue to human parasite S. stercoralis
  • L3 infectious stage
  • Penetrate skin, migration via lungs, mouth, intestine
  • Migration trace: exposure to chitin (material of cuticula)
    • trigger: transcription of IL-33

Immune Response agianst Helminth Infection

Timeline Infection Stage Immune Response
Day 0 - L3 infectious stage
- Penetrate skin
Day 2 - Migrate via skin, lung, to mouth.
- Invasion by swallow.		 - Eosinophilc and neutrophilic granulocytes.
- Fast mast cell degranulation promoted by IL-9.
Day 5 - Reach intestine.
- Moult to adult.		 - Fast mast cell degranulation promoted by IL-9.
Week 2 - Adaptive immune system established.
- Production of IgE and IgG, conventional activation of mast cell.
Week 3-4 - Full eradication.
Post-infection - Semi-resistant to subsequent infections
  • Mast cell-deficient mice are unable to terminate infections.

Helminth Defense

IL-33 triggers rapid parasite expulsion

  • Significant difference between control (PBS) and IL-33 application / IL-33 stabilization
  • IL-33 inhibitor (HpARI) increases parasite abundance

Activated mast cell excutes the extensive expulsion

  • mMCPT-1 (mast cell protease 1) reflects to which extent the mucosal mast cells have degranulated, measured with qPCR

Rapid activation in early stage by IL-9

  • Target of S. ratti infection strategy
    • Surpressing IL-9 mediated mast cell activation
      • Induction of Treg expansion (Blankenhaus et al. 2011)
      • Induction of negative regulatory receptors (BTLA) (Breloer et al. 2015)
  • IL-33 mediated IL-9 production by ILC2
